A Quick Q&A to Understanding Generative AI.

Generative AI is a branch of artificial intelligence that creates new content based on patterns it learned from data. It can produce text, images, videos, music, and even code. Instead of choosing from pre-existing templates, it generates original outputs depending on the prompt you give it. This makes it useful for writing assistance, creative work, problem-solving, simulations, and rapid idea generation.

Generative AI works by analyzing millions to billions of example data points and learning statistical patterns. When you ask it something, it predicts the most likely next words, shapes, or structures based on those patterns.

It's important to remember that it doesn’t think or understand context the way humans do. It doesn’t have beliefs, emotions, or real-world awareness. It produces answers based on probability, not personal experience or intuition. This is why it can sound confident even when it's wrong so use GenAI ethically and responsibly.

There are many tools, each with different strengths:
  • Chat-based AI - ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek
  • Image generators - MidJourney, Stable Diffusion, DALL·E
  • Video generators - Sora AI, Nano Banana, etc...
  • Coding assistants - GitHub Copilot, Codeium
Each tool specializes in a specific type of output, but all follow the same basic idea in generating content from learned patterns.

GenAI is strong in:
  • Conceptualizing - It helps produce concepts or ideas quickly when you’re stuck.
  • Summarizing - It can break down long texts into clearer explanations.
  • Drafting - Essays, cover letters, scripts, outlines, and reports can easily be drafted with GenAI.
  • Creative work - Images, character designs, poems, songs, story prompts can be created with GenAI but only do so when ethically done; see more at the ethics tab.
  • Coding assistance - Debugging, explaining code, generating examples, cleaning up your code, and finding a specific error within massive lines of code.
  • Language help - Grammar checking, rephrasing, translation are easily accessible with GenAI.
It works best when you're using it as a support tool rather than replacing your effort entirely, use it ETHICALLY.

GenAI's LIMITATIONS and RISKS
  • Incorrect answers - It can produce “confident but wrong” information or invent facts, this is very prominent so be careful.
  • Biases - AI models can reflect biases found in their training data, very prominent as well regarding information so always double-check.
  • Privacy risks - Sensitive personal info should never be typed into AI tools as your inputs themselves can be used as training data.
  • No real understanding - It can’t judge ethics, emotions, or context like a person can. Never fully rely on GenAI, be a human, think critically.
  • Academic integrity issues - Using AI improperly can count as plagiarism or misconduct, this is a big factor in todays world so we need to utilize GenAI properly.
  • Dependence - Overuse can weaken your critical thinking and writing skills, as said previously, over-reliance or dependence on GenAI can literally make you dumb and not think for yourself, so don't do this.
These risks are REAL; AI outputs must be checked, verified, and used responsibly ALWAYS.
