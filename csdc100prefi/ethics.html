Ethical Use of Generative AI

Guidelines for responsible, safe, and fair use of AI in school, research, and daily life.

Guidelines at a Glance

Why Ethics Matter

Ethical AI use ensures that technology enhances your learning and work without compromising honesty, fairness, or the wellbeing of others. Misuse can lead to misinformation, unfair advantage, or harm.

  • Maintain integrity. Use AI in a way that reflects your own understanding and effort.
  • Ensure fairness. Avoid giving yourself an unfair advantage in assignments, research, or collaborative work.
  • Respect others. Consider the impact of AI-generated content on peers, educators, and the wider community.
  • Reduce misinformation. Always verify AI outputs before using or sharing them.

Reference: UNESCO, Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023) — unesco.org

Responsible Use in the Academe

Using AI responsibly in academic settings means leveraging its strengths without compromising your learning, integrity, or the standards of your school. Here’s how to stay ethical and effective:

  • Assist, don’t replace. Use AI for brainstorming, summaries, or drafting ideas, but always ensure your understanding and original work shine through.
  • Follow the rules. Abide by classroom or institutional guidelines before using AI in assignments or projects.
  • Transparency matters. Clearly disclose whenever AI contributes to your work, whether in writing, editing, or research.
  • Verify everything. Always check facts, citations, and outputs from AI; it can make mistakes or present outdated information.
  • Protect your own privacy. Avoid sharing sensitive personal or institutional information with AI tools.
  • Think for yourself. Be smart, think critically. Don't overly rely on AI, you have a brain.

Reference: UNESCO, Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023) — unesco.org

Responsible Use in Research

AI can assist in research by summarizing data, generating drafts, or helping brainstorm ideas. However, your analysis, interpretations, and conclusions should remain original. Always cite AI tools when used, verify all outputs for accuracy, and be mindful of potential biases in AI-generated content.

Reference: UNESCO, Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023) — unesco.org

Responsible Use in Daily Life

In everyday life, AI can enhance productivity, help learn new skills, and assist with decision-making. Avoid relying on AI for medical, legal, or financial advice. Always cross-check information, recognize misinformation, and respect the privacy and rights of others.

Reference: OECD, AI Principles for Trustworthy Use (2023) — oecd.org

Good Habits When Using AI

Always verify AI-generated content, ask clarifying questions, and compare AI output with your own reasoning. Use multiple sources to confirm information and maintain a critical mindset. These habits help ensure AI complements rather than replaces your thinking.

Reference: UNESCO, Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023) — unesco.org

Key Takeaways

Ethical AI use means being responsible, checking facts, respecting privacy, and treating AI as a tool to enhance learning instead of a shortcut. Apply these principles consistently in school, research, and daily life.
